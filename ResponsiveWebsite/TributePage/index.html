Stanislav Petrov

The man who saved the world.

Significant Information

  • Born on 7th of September 1939 near Vladivostok in Russia to a WW2 aircraft fighter father and a nurse mother.
  • He enrolled at Kiev Higher Engineering Radio-Technical College and after graduating in 1972, joined the Soviet Air Defence Forces.
  • In the early 1970s, he was assigned to the organization that oversaw the new early warning system intended to detect ballistic missile attacks from NATO countries.
  • He played a key role in the 1983 Soviet nuclear false alarm incident in which the nuclear early-warning system of the Soviet Union failed and almost caused a nuclear war.
  • He received no reward. According to him, this was because the incident and other bugs found in the missile detection system embarrassed his superiors and the scientists who were responsible for it, so that if he had been officially rewarded, they would have had to be punished.
  • He died on 19th of May 2017 from hypostatic pneumonia.
